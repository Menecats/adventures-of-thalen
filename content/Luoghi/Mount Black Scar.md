Mount Black Scar is a tall mountain rising to about 2500 meters above sea level within the [[Tiger's Mouth]] mountain range. Although it is not the tallest peak in the region, it stands out for its distinctive dark, almost black coloration, caused by the unusual mineral that makes up much of its rock.

The mountain takes its name from its most striking feature: a massive ravine that cuts through the upper part of the peak. Beginning at the summit and running downward for nearly two-thirds the mountain’s height, this deep scar splits the mountain into two separate summits, giving the peak its distinctive silhouette.

At the base of the ravine lies a small settlement known as [[Cave Town]]. The inhabitants of this rugged village live primarily from quarrying the mountain's dark stone, which is extracted from the surrounding cliffs and exported to other regions. Many of the town's structures and workspaces are built directly into the rock itself.

Life on Mount Black Scar is harsh and sparse. The exposed slopes are constantly swept by strong winds, and the summits remain covered in snow throughout the entire year. Only a few hardy forms of life manage to survive in such an unforgiving environment.

Despite the presence of miners, the mountain retains an air of mystery. During particularly violent storms, some claim that deep, echoing howls rise from within the ravine. Many locals believe these sounds to be the distant cries of dragons, said to dwell somewhere in the depths of the scar.
