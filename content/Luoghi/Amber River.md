The Amber River is a slow and winding waterway that cuts through the heart of the [[Thornbush Forest]], shaping both the land and the lives of those who dwell nearby. Flowing from the high springs of the [[Tiger's Mouth]] mountains, its waters travel steadily westward, carving a natural corridor through the otherwise dense and tangled woodland.

The river takes its name from its distinctive coloration. In many stretches, especially where the current slows, the water reflects a deep amber hue, caused by the rich organic matter and tannins released from the surrounding vegetation. When struck by sunlight, the surface seems almost to glow, giving the river an otherworldly, subdued radiance.

Along its banks, the forest thins and the ground softens into fertile, moisture-rich soil. These rare clearings make the Amber River one of the few places in the region where agriculture can reliably flourish. Settlements such as [[Moonglave]] depend heavily on these lands, cultivating crops, fishing its waters, and using the river as a natural route for transport and communication.

Despite its generally calm appearance, the Amber River is not without danger. Its course is irregular, marked by sudden bends, submerged roots, and shifting banks that can make navigation difficult. In quieter sections, the water deepens unexpectedly, while in others it spreads into marshy flats where footing becomes treacherous. Wildlife is abundant along its edges, and not all of it is harmless.

For those who know how to follow it, the river offers one of the most reliable ways to move through the Thornbush Forest. For those who do not, it can just as easily become a slow and disorienting trap, leading them deeper into the wilderness rather than out of it.
