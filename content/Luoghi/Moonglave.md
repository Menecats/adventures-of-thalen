Moonglave is one of the largest settlements in the region, though it remains modest in scale compared to the great cities of the wider world. It lies deep within the [[Thornbush Forest]], where the dense woodland opens just enough to make room for cultivation and travel.

The town is built along the winding curves of the [[Amber River]], whose slow and steady waters shape both the land and the life of the settlement. Wide stretches of fertile soil extend along its banks, carefully cultivated into fields that sustain not only Moonglave itself but also smaller, more remote communities such as [[Lindle Creek]]. Crops grow in irregular patches carved out of the forest, where trees have been cleared but never fully driven back, leaving the settlement in a constant balance between growth and encroaching wilderness.

Unlike most places in the region, Moonglave is connected by one of the few reliable roads that cut through the forest. This makes it a vital crossroads for trade, where goods from the mountains, the deeper woods, and the distant lowlands converge. Caravans arrive regularly, carrying stone and minerals from [[Cave Town]], forest products from surrounding settlements, and essential supplies destined for the more isolated villages.

The town itself reflects this constant movement. Wooden structures line the main road and riverbanks, interspersed with storehouses, workshops, and open trading areas where merchants and travelers gather. Boats drift along the [[Amber River]], used for transport, fishing, and communication, further reinforcing Moonglave's role as a hub of exchange.

Despite its relative prosperity, Moonglave remains closely tied to the forest that surrounds it. The outer edges of the settlement fade quickly into dense vegetation, and beyond the well-trodden paths, the [[Thornbush Forest|Thornbush]] reclaims the land without hesitation. Those who live here understand that the town does not dominate the forest, but exists within it, sustained by its resources and constrained by its ever-present dangers.
